Shots fired on school grounds remain a problem in the U.S., but not as big a problem as it was two years ago

By: Caleb Winters

As of today, 2025 has seen 216 school shootings across the nation, according to the K-12 School Shooting Database. This is 120 fewer incidents than last year and 136 fewer incidents than Americans experienced in 2023.

K-12 School Shooting Database documents school shooting reports “when a gun is brandished, fired, or a bullet hits school property for any reason, regardless of the number of victims, time of day or day of the week,” according to its website.

The database recorded an incident on November 19, 2025, when an independent school district police officer accidentally discharged his firearm, injuring himself on Bowie Elementary School’s campus in Corsicana, Texas. The officer needed emergency medical care, but no students or staff were injured.

Another incident was recorded on the weekend of November 15, when a 911 caller reported hearing a gunshot and seeing a woman lying in the parking lot of Columbus Scioto 6 - 12. When the police confronted the woman, Janekqua Thornton, she claimed that she had tripped and fallen, accidentally discharging the firearm inside the bag she was carrying.

Violent school shootings, involving deaths and hostages, are also recorded in the school shooting database. On October 12, Tylar Jarod Goodloe, 18, had a cash bond set at more $2 million after being charged for the deadly shooting of two people at Heidelberg High School.

Rifles and handguns are recorded as the most significant types of guns used in school shootings in 2025.

Texas has seen 21 school shootings this year, the most incidents recorded in 2025. California, the second state with the highest record of incidents, has seen 18 school shootings. New York, Tennessee, and Ohio are tied for third, with all three states seeing 13 incidents.

Shootings that happen in university and college settings are not recorded in the school shooting database, though they have been some of the deadliest this year. On April 17, 2025, a mass shooting occurred at Florida State University, where Phoenix Ikner, a 20-year-old political science student, killed two and wounded six others.
